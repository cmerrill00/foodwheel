      Help & Features

      Eat Out vs. Cook at Home

      Use the Eat Out | Cook at Home toggle below the header to switch between two independent wheels. Each mode has its own food list, history, and filter chips. The settings panel applies to both modes.

      The Wheel

      The wheel randomly selects from your list when you click Spin the Wheel. The yellow triangle at the top is the pointer — whatever it lands on is the result. The button stays disabled until you have at least two options. If a filter is active, only the filtered options appear on the wheel.

      Adding Entries

      Click the + Add Location (Eat Out) or + Add Recipe (Cook at Home) button to open the entry form.

      Eat Out fields:

      • Name — restaurant name (required)
      • Category — optional grouping label, e.g. "Fast Food" or "Italian"
      • Website URL — if provided, the name becomes a clickable link; https:// is added automatically if omitted
      • Drive-thru / Online ordering / Dine-in only — feature flags used for filtering
      • Weight % — relative spin probability (default 100). 200 = twice as likely, 50 = half as likely.

      Cook at Home fields:

      • Name — recipe or meal name (required)
      • Category — optional grouping label, e.g. "Pasta" or "Soup"
      • Cook time — estimated minutes; used for the time-range filter chips above the wheel
      • ⚡ Quick / 🕐 Slow Cook / 🔆 Oven/Bake / 🔥 Grill / 🍲 One Pot / 🥗 No Cook — feature flags for filtering
      • Weight % — relative spin probability (default 100)

      Managing Your List

      Each entry shows three icons on the right:

      • 📝 — Opens the notes panel. The icon turns gold when any notes content has been saved.
      • ✏️ — Opens an inline editor for all fields. Press Enter or the save icon to confirm, or ✕ to cancel.
      • ✕ — Permanently removes the entry.

      Notes

      Click the 📝 icon on any entry to open its notes panel.

      • Eat Out: a single freeform text area for anything worth remembering — favorite dishes, past experiences, recommendations.
      • Cook at Home: three labeled sections — Ingredients, Instructions, and Notes. Use them to store a full recipe alongside anything extra.

      Click outside the panel, press Escape, or use Cancel to close without saving.

      Filtering the Wheel

      Filter chips appear above the wheel once you have categories or feature flags set:

      • Row 1 — Categories: select one or more to limit the wheel to those groups (OR logic). Tap All to clear.
      • Row 2 — Features: all selected feature chips must match (AND logic). Chips only appear when at least one entry has that feature.
      • Row 3 — Cook time (Cook at Home only): ≤ 30 min, 31–60 min, or 60+ min. Tap the active chip again to clear. Only one range can be active at a time.

      Pick History

      Every spin is logged with date and time. Each mode has its own history view. From the list you can:

      • Check the checkbox to mark an entry as eaten/cooked. Confirmed entries are struck through and count toward the weighting system.
      • Use Mark as Eaten below the spin result to confirm without scrolling.
      • Click ✕ to remove individual history entries.
      • Use Clear History in the ⚙ settings panel to wipe all history (both modes).

      Spin Weight

      Each entry has a Weight % field (1–999, default 100) that scales its slice on the wheel proportionally. A 200% entry gets twice the slice of a 100% entry; a 50% entry gets half. When recency reduction is also active, the penalty is applied on top of the per-entry weight — a 500% entry reduced by 50% lands at 250%.

      Reducing Recent Repeats (Weighting)

      Open the ⚙ panel and enable Reduce chance of recently eaten foods. Two values control how it works:

      • Reduce by X% — how much to lower the probability for a recently confirmed entry.
      • Within the last X days — how far back to look. Only confirmed (checked) entries within this window are counted.

      Slice sizes on the wheel update visually to reflect adjusted probabilities. Click Save to apply.

      Backup & Restore

      All options are in the ⚙ settings panel under Backup & Restore:

      • Export Config — downloads a JSON file containing both your Eat Out and Cook at Home lists plus settings. History is not included.
      • Import Config — loads a previously exported file, replacing your current lists and settings.
      • Load Defaults — replaces your Eat Out list with 34 pre-configured US restaurant chains.
      • Clear Config — wipes all food entries and resets settings. Offers a backup download first.
      • Clear History — removes all pick history for both modes.
